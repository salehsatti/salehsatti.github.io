Completed Research

The Sudan Hydroeconomic Optimization Model (SHOM)

This research uses GAMS to build a non-linear optimization model that aims of the model is to maximize competing tradeoffs between hydropower production and irrigation along the Sudanese Blue Nile. Details of the model, as well as a sensitivity analysis that provides further insight to Sudanese management strategies under changing basin conditions can be found here.

Ongoing Research

Improving Seasonal Forecasting

Understanding East African regional dynamics can help provide more accurate short range climate forecasts for food insecure regions within the Greater Horn of Africa (GHA). Use of climate regionalization can help isolate various climate phenomena that influences seasonal precipitation within the region. Results of this study will help improve the current understanding of regional drivers of precipitation within various regions of GHA, and across various operational statistical and dynamical forecasting models.

Using water resources optimization methods to assess value of Blue Nile Seasonal Forecasts

By using forecasted flows as upstream boundary conditions into SHOM, this research will:
1. Assess the theoretical value of a seasonal forecast
2. Evaluate the trade off between of lag time of forecast and the value of forecast for downstream decision makers.
3. Help highlight the difference between accurate forecasts and valuable forecasts with the Blue Nile Basin.

Empirical Downscaling and Interpolation of Tunisian Precipitation

By using machine learning algorithms, and geostatistical interpolations spatial climate maps can be created in order to generate downscaled climate projections in areas with inadequate (and often times unreliable) climate data records.
